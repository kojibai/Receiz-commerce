# Wilds Hearttree Real-Card Expedition Design

**Date:** 2026-07-16  
**Status:** Approved design  
**Scope:** First production vertical slice for the wider Wilds location, mini-game, progression, and official-audio program

## 1. Objective

Replace the current Hearttree button sequence with a complete 10–15 minute authored systemic expedition in which the player's exact verified cards determine the playable space, available actions, challenge structure, tactical options, risks, rewards, and long-term consequences.

The vertical slice establishes reusable production systems for every later Wilds location:

- exact card-revision admission;
- real stat, ability, element, anatomy, lineage, and condition effects;
- one-to-three-card strategic squads;
- deterministic dynamic challenges;
- contribution-based experience and bounded upgrades;
- persistent injury, exhaustion, mastery, and irreversible death;
- server-replayable outcomes and atomic Receiz admission;
- locally shipped official music, sound effects, ambience, and properly recorded human dialogue.

Hearttree is the quality standard, not a generic template that later locations merely reskin. Arena, Prism Arcade, Wayfinder Hollow, ecology activities, bosses, and future destinations reuse the trusted foundations while receiving distinct mechanics.

## 2. Player Promise

> Choose the right living cards, understand what each can truly do, play them skillfully under pressure, survive a sanctuary that changes in response to the squad, and leave with consequences that permanently belong to those cards.

The result must satisfy four conditions:

1. Card selection materially changes the expedition.
2. Player execution materially changes the outcome.
3. The expedition materially changes the cards.
4. Every lasting outcome has an explainable, replayable cause.

There is no hidden roll that arbitrarily decides success, injury, reward, or death.

## 3. Experience Structure

### 3.1 Squad Gate

Each challenge declares an allowed and required squad size. Hearttree supports one, two, or three admitted cards. Later challenges may require larger parties through the same admission model.

Admission may require:

- a minimum or maximum card count;
- verified ownership;
- a specific element or combination of elements;
- physical capabilities derived from anatomy;
- an ability tag or tactical role;
- stage, bond, mastery, lineage, achievement, or prior history;
- acceptable health, injury, exhaustion, and playable-life state.

The game pins the exact proof revision of every admitted card. It explains each card's relevant strengths, liabilities, and known interactions without revealing puzzle solutions. The squad cannot be silently exchanged after admission.

### 3.2 Rootway Exploration

The player directly controls a lead card through a collision-based 3D sanctuary. The Rootway contains moving roots, elevation, narrow passages, reactive flora, hazards, memory traces, optional routes, and hidden chambers.

Support cards expose active tactical abilities. The player may switch the lead card during safe windows or spend a limited tactical resource to switch during danger. Traversal and exploration respond to real capabilities rather than cosmetic card categories.

### 3.3 Memory Trial

The trial combines navigation, observation, timing, positioning, and card coordination. Puzzle families include:

- redirecting moonlight and elemental energy;
- crossing reactive root networks;
- tracing lineage memories;
- stabilizing or breaking living structures;
- protecting a moving objective;
- interrupting a ritual sequence;
- revealing and exploiting a hidden route.

Challenges require decisions and execution. They cannot be completed by pressing an enabled action button in a predetermined order.

### 3.4 Root Master

The finale is a multi-phase tactical encounter with:

- readable attack telegraphs;
- real positioning and collision;
- attack ranges and areas;
- dodge, guard, stagger, interrupt, and counter windows;
- stamina, cooldown, exposure, health, and switching costs;
- elemental and environmental interactions;
- squad-specific boss adaptations;
- arena changes caused by earlier expedition choices.

The player wins by reading and adapting. Repeating one optimal input is not a viable universal strategy.

### 3.5 Heart Choice

The standard exit grants contribution experience and a bounded upgrade choice. Optional hidden Mortal branches disclose permanent-death risk and offer distinctive mastery, history, cosmetic, reputation, and ability-expression opportunities.

Players may extract instead of continuing. The game never disguises a Mortal path as an ordinary challenge.

## 4. Exact Card Simulation

### 4.1 Canonical Inputs

The Card Capability Projector reads the exact verified card revision, including:

- health, power, guard, speed, and bond;
- element and species;
- ability names and their authored mechanical definitions;
- body, locomotion, appendages, surface, aura, and behavioral traits;
- stage, ascension, rarity, and foil where mechanically justified;
- growth paths, achievements, quests, and mastery;
- lineage, parents, children, and prior Hearttree history;
- current injuries, exhaustion, scars, and life state.

Display rarity or foil never grants an unexplained universal advantage. A field affects play only through an authored, player-readable rule.

### 4.2 Stat Meaning

- **Health:** endurance, injury thresholds, and committed-action survival.
- **Power:** physical force, ability output, guard damage, and environmental manipulation.
- **Guard:** mitigation, stagger resistance, shielding, and structural stability.
- **Speed:** acceleration, dodge distance, initiative, switching time, and reaction-window width.
- **Bond:** command responsiveness, support coordination, recovery efficiency, and combo reliability.

High statistics create stronger opportunities and wider margins. They do not replace player decisions or guarantee success.

### 4.3 Physical and Elemental Identity

Anatomy has real physical consequences:

- flying cards cross gaps and reach elevated targets;
- serpentine cards enter narrow root channels;
- armored and shelled cards resist crushing pressure;
- horns can break or redirect structures;
- tails can balance, manipulate, or anchor;
- wings, crests, ears, and aura profiles create authored sensory and movement capabilities.

Elements have consistent world rules. Grove binds and restores, Spark energizes and overloads, Tide redirects and cleanses, and Stone anchors and breaks. Every additional catalog element receives its own authored interaction grammar before it is used in a challenge.

### 4.4 Ability Registry

Every real ability name resolves through a versioned registry containing:

- mechanical tags;
- valid targets;
- range and area;
- wind-up, active, and recovery timing;
- stamina or tactical-resource cost;
- cooldown;
- stat scaling;
- environmental effects;
- counters and interruption rules;
- support and combo behavior;
- audiovisual event identifiers.

Unknown abilities fail closed during admission. They do not receive a fabricated generic effect.

### 4.5 Outcome Resolution

The conceptual outcome model is:

`outcome = card action potential × matchup × positioning × timing × tactical state`

All factors are deterministic and recorded. Player skill includes movement, aim, timing, target choice, sequencing, resource management, telegraph recognition, switching, extraction judgment, and squad construction.

A weaker well-matched card played skillfully can outperform a powerful mismatched card. An exceptional card can be injured or killed through poor positioning and decisions.

## 5. Dynamic Expedition Director

The Expedition Director derives a reproducible run from:

- the sanctuary seed and generator version;
- pinned squad proof revisions;
- prior Hearttree history;
- player and card mastery;
- accepted injuries and conditions;
- selected difficulty and explicit Mortal consent.

It selects and configures:

- chamber topology and routes;
- traversal constraints;
- hazards and timings;
- puzzle grammar;
- enemy composition and behavior;
- Root Master phases and counters;
- secrets and optional Mortal paths;
- card-specific mastery opportunities;
- adaptive musical layers and motifs.

The environment reacts to the squad. Flying cards may expose aerial conductors; Stone guardians may reveal breakable routes and displacement threats; Tide and Grove combinations may create water-growth systems; lineage-connected squads may open ancestral chambers.

Normal expeditions must have at least one valid solution using the admitted squad and player skill. The director validates solvability before signing the definition. It may test weaknesses but cannot generate an impossible normal run. Mortal branches may exceed safe capability only when their danger is disclosed and entry is voluntary.

Repeated trivial solutions yield diminishing experience. Taking a new role, solving a harder variant, protecting another card, or mastering a different tactical route creates fresh growth opportunities.

## 6. Roles and Squad Strategy

Roles are derived capabilities, not permanent classes:

- **Vanguard:** absorbs pressure, guards allies, and breaks heavy roots.
- **Pathfinder:** reveals routes, reads traps, and creates movement openings.
- **Channeler:** redirects energy, changes elemental states, and interrupts rituals.
- **Warden:** shields, cleanses, and anchors unstable chambers.
- **Striker:** exploits openings and commits high-output actions.
- **Resonant:** interacts with memories, lineage locks, and secrets.

A card may fulfill multiple roles with different efficiency. Challenges reward complementary squad construction while preserving alternative solutions. There is no universal best card or mandatory single composition across the whole sanctuary.

## 7. Experience, Upgrades, and Persistent Condition

### 7.1 Contribution Experience

Cards earn experience only for recorded contribution, including:

- discovering or creating a route;
- solving a mechanism;
- protecting another card;
- absorbing or avoiding meaningful pressure;
- interrupting or countering an enemy;
- exploiting a weakness;
- supporting a combo;
- completing an objective;
- surviving an extraction;
- finishing a phase.

Idle support cards receive little or no experience. Duplicate events and trivial farming are rejected or diminished.

### 7.2 Bounded Upgrade Choices

Accepted thresholds offer choices such as:

- a new legal ability expression;
- an existing ability specialization;
- role mastery;
- bond growth;
- feat-derived cosmetic evolution;
- eligibility for a later Ascension path.

Upgrades append to living history. They do not silently rewrite a prior proof revision.

### 7.3 Escalating Consequences

1. Fatigue limits current-run action capacity.
2. Exhaustion persists until recovered through gameplay.
3. Injury creates a specific persistent limitation or vulnerability.
4. Critical condition makes extraction the safe choice.
5. Explicit Mortal failure can cause permanent death.

Injuries have known causes and recovery paths. They are not arbitrary timers.

## 8. Permanent Death

Death is permanent and irreversible.

Before a Mortal challenge, the game presents:

- the exact cards at risk;
- known hazards and survival conditions;
- current card condition;
- the irreversible-death rule;
- available extraction or prepared survival options;
- the reward and mastery categories at stake.

The player explicitly confirms the pinned cards and risk. A valid death event records the challenge, cause, defending actions, witnesses, transcript reference, and final card revision.

A dead card:

- remains visible in history and memorials;
- cannot battle, enter activities, trade, breed, revive, or be restored from an older client copy;
- cannot be relabeled as merely injured or unavailable;
- may affect future sanctuary memories, descendants, world monuments, and player reputation.

Network failure, browser failure, rejected submission, stale state, or server error can never kill a card.

## 9. Authority and Data Flow

### 9.1 Components

- **Card Capability Projector:** verified revision to playable capability model.
- **Ability Registry:** versioned authored ability mechanics.
- **Expedition Director:** deterministic and solvable expedition definition.
- **Challenge Runtime:** fixed-step movement, collision, hazards, objectives, and combat.
- **Action Resolver:** deterministic effect calculation.
- **Consequence Engine:** experience, injury, mastery, upgrade, extraction, and death projection.
- **Transcript and Replay:** bounded inputs, important states, outcomes, and digests.
- **Receiz Authority Adapter:** ownership pinning, checkpoint admission, replay validation, and atomic append.
- **Presentation Layer:** 3D environment, characters, HUD, input, accessibility, and feedback.
- **Adaptive Audio Director:** local asset loading, musical state, spatial sound, dialogue, mixing, and pause/resume.

### 9.2 Run Lifecycle

1. Verify ownership and playable life state.
2. Pin every selected card revision.
3. Generate and sign a deterministic expedition definition.
4. Simulate responsive play locally and record bounded inputs.
5. Admit important checkpoints.
6. Replay and validate the submitted result server-side.
7. Atomically append all accepted consequences.
8. Publish new card revisions and run history only after admission succeeds.

Presentation may respond immediately, but it cannot declare a permanent reward, injury, upgrade, or death canonical before admission.

### 9.3 Recovery and Failure Handling

- Disconnects resume from the last admitted checkpoint.
- Duplicate commands and submissions are idempotent.
- Stale card revisions require reconciliation before continuing.
- Invalid transcripts grant no reward and apply no consequence.
- Partial reward or death mutations are prohibited.
- An unavailable audio asset degrades to a documented silent event without changing simulation.
- Accessibility modes may widen presentation cues but cannot change authoritative timing unless the selected difficulty explicitly defines that rule.

## 10. Official Audio Production

Hearttree ships finished local audio assets. Browser oscillator tones and planned-but-missing files are not acceptable production content.

### 10.1 Source Policy

- Original internally composed music using real sampled instruments and layered performances.
- Original designed sound effects and performed material sounds.
- License-audited CC0 field recordings only where they improve realism.
- Human dialogue recorded by real performers with documented rights.
- Open-source offline production tools are allowed.
- No runtime cloud service, API, generated browser tone, or third-party playback dependency.

Each asset receives an entry containing authorship, source, license, edits, format, duration, loop points, loudness, peak, bus, triggers, and version.

### 10.2 Hearttree Audio Set

- exterior and interior sanctuary ambience;
- exploration, mystery, danger, mastery, boss, victory, extraction, and memorial music stems;
- card-element and lineage motif layers;
- footsteps and movement by surface and anatomy;
- roots, leaves, wood strain, stone, water, energy, and mechanisms;
- ability, guard, dodge, switch, impact, injury, reward, extraction, and death effects;
- spatial enemy and Root Master sounds;
- human-performed narrator and Root Master dialogue once recorded and rights-cleared;
- UI confirm, warning, invalid, Mortal consent, checkpoint, and admission feedback.

The score changes from gameplay events and squad identity. It does not merely switch one full track when combat begins.

### 10.3 Runtime Behavior

- User gesture unlocks audio.
- Music, ambience, effects, creatures, dialogue, and UI have separate buses.
- Pause, backgrounding, mute, and resume preserve deterministic gameplay state.
- Dialogue ducking remains subtle and configurable.
- Spatial sounds use bounded concurrency, distance falloff, and priority.
- Missing or failed audio is surfaced in diagnostics without blocking gameplay.

## 11. Interface and Accessibility

The HUD exposes information needed for skillful decisions:

- active and support cards;
- exact health, condition, stamina, cooldowns, and switch readiness;
- enemy telegraphs and known effects;
- target, range, and environmental interaction state;
- current objective and optional danger;
- extraction availability;
- Mortal status and cards at risk.

Desktop and touch controls support movement, aim, primary and secondary abilities, guard or dodge, switching, interaction, and extraction without overlapping the playable field.

Required accessibility support includes keyboard navigation, remappable controls, touch-safe targets, reduced motion, color-independent signals, captions, visualized critical audio cues, separate volume controls, mute-safe play, and readable timing indicators.

## 12. Verification and Completion Criteria

### 12.1 Rules and Determinism

- Unit tests cover every stat, element, anatomy capability, role, ability rule, status, and consequence.
- Property tests prove bounded values, deterministic replay, idempotency, and no partial mutation.
- Solvability tests cover representative one-, two-, and three-card squads.
- Replay tests reproduce accepted results and reject tampered inputs.
- Death tests prove that only valid explicitly accepted Mortal states can append death.
- Recovery tests prove that network and browser failures cannot kill cards.

### 12.2 Player-Facing Quality

- Representative squads produce materially different routes, challenges, boss behavior, audio, and reward opportunities.
- Real card fields visibly and measurably affect play.
- Player timing, positioning, switching, and resource choices change success.
- At least two valid solutions exist for representative normal challenge families.
- The complete run targets 10–15 minutes.
- Repeated runs with materially different squads remain strategically distinct.
- Experience, upgrades, injuries, extraction, and death have explainable causes in the result transcript.

### 12.3 Production Gates

- Typecheck, lint, unit, integration, property, replay, and production build pass.
- Desktop and mobile browser playtests complete the main loop and fail/extract path.
- Keyboard and touch inputs are verified.
- Console and page errors are clear.
- Frame time, memory, audio decoding, and asset budgets pass on target mobile hardware.
- Every referenced production audio file exists, decodes, and matches its manifest.
- Loudness, loop seams, spatial behavior, pause/resume, and mute behavior pass review.
- No oscillator-tone placeholder or unlicensed production asset remains.

## 13. Program Sequence After Hearttree

After Hearttree satisfies the vertical-slice gates, the wider program proceeds in this order:

1. Rebuild Arena of Echoes around competitive tactical play.
2. Rebuild Prism Arcade around distinct skill-based cooperative mini-games.
3. Rebuild Wayfinder Hollow around consequential services, relationships, routes, and civic progression.
4. Replace all eight ecology button sequences with distinct systemic activities.
5. Integrate bosses, multiplayer consequences, long-term progression, and reward balance.
6. Complete cross-location audio, human cast, playtesting, tuning, and release qualification.

No later location may regress to a predetermined button sequence or treat cards as cosmetic admission tokens.
